Albert Einstein


Born in 1879 in Ulm, Germany, was a towering figure in the world of
physics.His groundbreaking theories revolutionized our
understanding of the universe. In 1905, he published his theory of
special relativity, which introduced the famous equation E=mcÂ²,
revealing the equivalence of energy and mass.

Einstein's work paved the way for modern physics, fundamentally altering concepts of space and time. In 1915, he developed the theory of general relativity, explaining the gravitational force as the bending of spacetime by massive objects.

Einstein's genius extended beyond theory; he received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect. His legacy transcends science, inspiring
countless minds and leaving an indelible mark on human understanding of the cosmos.
